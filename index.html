Classification Settings
New to ground classification? The defaults work well for most drone LiDAR surveys — just hit Run. If results aren't quite right, use the tips below each slider to adjust.
Grid Cell Size
2.0 m
Size of each ground surface tile.
↓ Smaller → more terrain detail, slower
↑ Larger → smoother result, faster
Try 1–3 m for hilly terrain, 3–6 m for flat fields.
Max Ground Thickness
0.30 m
How far above bare earth a point can be and still be ground.
↓ Lower → only bare soil (strict)
↑ Higher → includes low grass & gravel
Too many buildings classified? Lower this value.
Surface Smoothness
3
How smooth the computed ground surface looks.
↓ Low → follows rough terrain closely
↑ High → gentle, rolling surface
Ground has holes or spikes? Increase this.
Accuracy Passes
2
How many times the algorithm self-corrects.
↓ 1 pass → fast, good for most cases
↑ 3–4 passes → more accurate, slower
Large files? Start with 1–2 passes.
